3.2 Power-off

![](../../../../_images/01.01%20QAM%208255%20Power%20Dequence/image-2023-11-9_17-54-6.png)

■For power-off sequence, see System Power States.

■In normal operation, a graceful power-off sequence should be triggered by VIP sending an interrupt to QAM8255P Main Domain. Within QAM8255P, SA8255P will pull down PMA_PS_HOLD to initiate PMIC POFF.

□At first, critical power rails are turned off in sequence to meet SoC and memory device (UFS and LPDDR5) requirements

□Remaining power rails are turned off together afterwards

■In an event when VDD_VSYS is suddenly removed, PMM8650AUs will detect VDD_VSYS below threshold and trigger power off.

□All power rails in the system are turned off at the same time. Uncontrollable power off may cause damage on QAM8255P. For details, see Sudden Power Loss

■In an event when system hangs due to SA8255P or PMM8650AUs, a fast power off sequence should be triggered by VIP pulling FAST_POFF_EN_N (PMA_GPIO_4) low.

□If PMM8650AUs are still responsive, the same graceful power-off sequence as normal operation would be implemented to reliably turn off QAM8255P

□If PMM8650AUs are not responsive in 10ms, VIP will trigger a stage 3 reset on PMM8650AUs by pulling PM_RESIN_N low. VDD_VSYS power removal can be followed if PMICs remain unresponsive. Uncontrollable power off may cause damage on QAM8255P

3.5 Sudden Power Loss

■During a sudden power loss event, to guarantee all components in QAM8255P are powered off in a controlled sequence, Qualcomm Technologies, Inc. (QTI) recommends maintaining VDD_VSYS supply above minimal voltage requirement for at least 4ms. Otherwise, potentially QAM8255P and storage devices may get damaged over time.

□For QAM8255P with UFS3.x being used, the minimal voltage requirement is 2.8V

□For QAM8255P with UFS2.x being used, the minimal voltage requirement is 3.135V

□It is the responsibility of the customer to monitor VDD_VSYS for sudden power loss event and generate an interrupt to QAM8255P PMA_GPIO_4 when such an event occurs.

□The 4ms requirement starts after QAM8255P receives this interrupt.

□The total energy required at VDD_VSYS for 4ms varies based on the worst-case power consumption defined by the system, an example would be TDP70. The power consumption will dramatically drop once SoC is in reset stage which happens about 110μs after FAST_POFF_EN_N is received by PMICs. The current consumption at VDD_VSYS of the QAM8255P is about 2A once SoC is in reset state based on the measurement with the worst condition (high leakage part running at 115°C Tj). Customer is encouraged to determine the capacitance value based on the real measurement from the system with margins.

■Alternatively, customers may eliminate the need for 4ms energy on VDD_VSYS if the total number of sudden power loss event can be limited under 400 times.

□Per JEDEC, LPDDR5 devices should be able to tolerate 400 times of uncontrolled power-off over the lifetime.

□QTI has identified certain IPs inside SoC which may be vulnerable during specific power down conditions and the likelihood of occurrence of such conditions is extremely low during an un-commanded or sudden power loss event. Based on the statistical data from internal tests, the risk of damaging the SoC after 400 sudden power loss events is negligible.

□To ensure that all the PMICs turn off around the same time, ramp down VDD_VSYS from 2.75V to 1V in less than 50ms.

■There are no clear requirements from industry standards for UFS and NOR flash devices to tolerate sudden power loss events. It is the responsibility of the customer to work with memory vendors to address this concern, if applicable.
