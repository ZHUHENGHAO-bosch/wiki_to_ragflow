### Cockpit ↔ Vehicle Domain Interactions

| Cockpit Output | Consumer Domain | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Driver drowsy event | ADAS | Increase TTC margin, enable lane-keep nudges |
| "Open window" voice command | Body / Comfort | Window motor actuates |
| Personal profile recognized | Chassis | Seat/mirror/steering position recalled |
| AR-HUD lane guidance | ADAS perception | Receives lane lines, objects |
| Emergency call ("eCall") | Telematics / 5G | Connects to emergency services |
| Health anomaly (heart rate) | Cloud + ADAS | Suggest pull-over, contact family |
| OTA model update | Central compute / Gateway | Validated and deployed |

### Functional Safety concept

The AI Cockpit Functional Safety Concept combines

- ISO 26262 ASIL-B integrity for the cluster/HMI/telltales
- ISO 21448 (SOTIF) for AI-driven functions like DMS/OMS
- ISO/PAS 8800 for the underlying AI/ML lifecycle

### Potential Harards

H-01: Misinterpretation of safety-critical voice or gesture commands AI executes a command different from what the driver intended (e.g., "cancel call" heard as "cancel cruise").

H-02: Unintended deactivation of active safety / driver-assistance features Voice, LLM, or personalization AI turns off ADAS, lane-keep, or warnings without authorized driver intent.

H-03: Erroneous activation of comfort or body features during critical driving AI opens window, folds mirror, repositions seat, or starts a call at an unsafe moment.

H-04: System failure due to AI workload overload on shared SoC LLM / generative-AI workload starves NPU/CPU/thermal budget, degrading other cockpit functions.

H-05: Delayed or absent response to safety-critical user interaction AI latency, network dependency, or queueing causes safety-relevant input/output to arrive too late.

H-06: Faulty driver-state interpretation DMS misjudges drowsiness, distraction, gaze, or readiness — either false negative or false positive.

H-07: Unintended vehicle motion triggered by AI commands Voice/LLM/agent calls into ADAS, powertrain, or chassis cause unexpected acceleration, braking, or steering.

H-08: Persistent performance degradation due to AI model drift Real-world distribution diverges from training data → silent decline in field performance.

H-09: System crash or unsafe behavior due to AI software fault Bug, memory leak, or instability in the AI runtime/framework affects cockpit availability.

H-10: Incorrect prioritization between AI features and safety functions Hypervisor / scheduler / arbitration logic favors AI tasks over safety-critical HMI tasks.

H-11: Hallucinated or incorrect information presented to the driver LLM, AR-HUD, or assistant provides factually wrong safety-relevant guidance (navigation, vehicle status, advice).

H-12: Unintended action triggered by adversarial or external input Prompt injection (Bluetooth metadata, OCR'd text, ambient audio) or spoofed input causes AI to act unsafely.

H-13: Misleading or missing AR-HUD safety overlay Ghost overlay, missed object, or misregistered guidance causes driver to make wrong decision.

H-14: Wrong occupant or driver classification Identity, profile, or occupant-class AI applies the wrong settings or sends wrong data to other domains.

H-15: Driver over-trust or distraction induced by AI behavior Anthropomorphic voice, proactive notifications, verbose responses, or mode confusion reduces driver vigilance or eyes-on-road time.

H-16: Loss of safety-critical AI function due to sensor degradation Dirty lens, blocked mic, faulty IR illuminator → AI continues operating on bad input without detection.

H-17: Unsafe fleet-wide behavior after OTA AI model update New model regresses on edge cases, behaves unpredictably, or is silently incompatible with existing data pipeline.

H-18: Unauthorized vehicle control via AI interface Voice biometric spoof, replay, or LLM jailbreak grants control to an unauthorized user.

H-19: Inappropriate cross-domain action triggered by cockpit AI Emotion/health/context AI influences ADAS, body, or chassis behavior in a way not anticipated by the driver.

H-20: Privacy or data-handling failure with safety implications AI-mediated data leakage between profiles/occupants or to cloud causes safety-relevant harm (custody, stalking, social-engineering attack surface).
